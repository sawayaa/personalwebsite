Who I am

A biochemist turned swim coach turned mechanical engineer, I have embarked on a diverse and exciting professional journey. Following my graduation from Kenyon College with a B.A. in Biochemistry, I pursued my passion for competitive swimming by coaching at esteemed institutions such as UCSD and MIT, as well as various club teams. However, desiring a change in my career path while embracing my childhood passion for building, I decided to explore the field of engineering.

Subsequently, I enrolled in the engineering post-baccalaureate program at Boston University known as LEAP. During my time at BU, I had the opportunity to contribute to a research paper for Dr. Rebecca Khurshid's CAIR lab, where we focused on the development of wireless haptic robots. This experience deepened my interest in engineering and motivated me to pursue further education.

Hence, I transferred to Columbia University, where I am currently pursuing a Master of Science in mechanical engineering with a specialization in robotics and controls. My ultimate goal is to become a space roboticist, as I am fascinated by the challenges and possibilities that lie within this field. However, I am also open to diverse experiences that allow me to enhance my engineering skills and contribute to innovative projects.

Outside of my professional pursuits, I enjoy engaging in side hobbies such as coding, creating websites for individuals, as well as participating in activities like swimming, surfing, and sailing. These endeavors provide me with a well-rounded perspective and fuel my creativity and problem-solving abilities.

I invite you to explore my portfolio below for a comprehensive overview of my work. Please feel free to reach out to me if you have any questions or would like to discuss potential opportunities. I am eager to contribute my skills and passion to exciting projects and make a meaningful impact in the field of mechanical engineering.
